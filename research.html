Research

Nano optics, ultrafast spectroscopy, optical frequency comb metrology.

I develop nano-optical methods to observe and control optical and vibrational modes below the diffraction limit, with emphasis on surface phonon polaritons and plasmon polaritons, near-field interferometry, and nanoscale spectroscopy.

  • s-SNOM, nano-FTIR, and hyperspectral nano-imaging across Mid-IR to THz
  • Surface phonon polaritons (SPhPs) and surface plasmon polaritons (SPPs) in 2D materials
  • Strong and ultrastrong light-matter coupling in nanophotonic resonators
  • Real-space dispersion mapping and polaritonic interferometry
  • Development of cryogenic and time-resolved near-field measurement techniques
  • Nano-metrology for semiconductor and quantum materials characterization

I build and apply ultrafast and frequency comb-based measurement approaches to access dynamics and precision spectroscopy, bridging time-domain pump-probe methods with broadband coherent metrology.

  • Pump-probe nanospectroscopy for ultrafast carrier and phonon dynamics
  • Optical frequency comb metrology and high-resolution coherent spectroscopy
  • Dual-comb spectroscopy and hyperspectral digital holography
  • Precision timing, phase control, and broadband mid-infrared sources
  • Hyperspectral data analysis pipelines and signal processing for physics
  • Nonlinear optics and supercontinuum generation in photonic structures
