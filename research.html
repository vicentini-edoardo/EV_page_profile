Research

Nano-optics, ultrafast spectroscopy, and frequency-comb metrology with a broad point of view on surface light-matter interaction.

I design instruments that connect nanoscale optical imaging with absolute frequency and ultrafast timing. The goal is to reveal how polaritons, phonons, and plasmons evolve in real materials, then translate those measurements into control strategies for photonic devices.

Ultrafast nano-spectroscopy

Combining femtosecond pump-probe excitation with near-field microscopy, I follow energy flow in nanoscale structures beyond the diffraction limit. These measurements capture ultrafast relaxation and provide design rules for active materials.

Focus: transient vibrational contrast, nanoscale heat flow, coherent phonon dynamics.

Heterodyne s-SNOM with phase-resolved detection and lock-in demodulation at higher harmonics enables amplitude/phase transients. I implement femtosecond timing stabilization, synchronized pump-probe gating, and phase cycling for background rejection.

Focus: time-resolved nano-FTIR, coherent phonon-polariton mapping, few-cycle timing control.

Frequency-comb metrology

Frequency combs bring absolute accuracy and speed to nano-spectroscopy. I adapt comb-based measurements to near-field probes, enabling rapid nanoscale spectra that remain traceable to primary standards.

Focus: precision spectroscopy, high-throughput spectral mapping, traceable calibration.

Dual-comb interferometry is adapted to scattering-type near-field detection by phase-locking combs to a common reference. The acquisition pipeline uses coherent averaging, resampling, and calibration transfer for comb-to-FTIR consistency.

Focus: RF-to-optical mapping, timing jitter cancellation, frequency-accurate nano-FTIR.

Polaritons & strong coupling

I investigate how light couples to collective material excitations in nanocavities and surfaces. The work targets new regimes of polariton control, including ultrastrong coupling and hybrid platforms.

Focus: dispersion engineering, surface wave control, coupling strength maps.

Near-field mapping resolves polariton propagation vectors and lifetime. Coupled-mode analysis quantifies Rabi splitting and non-perturbative coupling. Spectral scans track dispersion under tunable cavity detuning.

Focus: polariton dispersion extraction, mode volume analysis, ultrastrong coupling diagnostics.
