Areas of expertise

Three complementary toolkits — from laser engineering and metrological precision to nanometre-scale spatial resolution.

01

Laser physics & nonlinear optics

Design and exploitation of ultrafast solid-state and fibre laser sources, and nonlinear frequency conversion, for coherent light from the near- to mid-infrared.

  • Kerr-lens mode-locked Cr:ZnSe femtosecond lasers (~2.4 µm, the “Ti:sapphire of the mid-IR”)
  • Er:fibre frequency combs and self-referenced stabilization
  • Coherent mid-IR supercontinuum in chalcogenide (As–Se) fibres
  • Cavity-enhanced and Vernier-filtered direct-comb spectroscopy
02

Frequency-comb metrology & spectroscopy

Optical frequency combs as phase-coherent “rulers” of light — enabling SI-traceable spectroscopy and coherent imaging without mechanical scanning.

  • Absolute optical-frequency stabilization to molecular references (~10 kHz linewidth)
  • Direct frequency-comb molecular spectroscopy with scanning micro-cavity resonators
  • Dual-comb spectroscopy: broadband, scan-free, MHz-resolution acquisition
  • Dual-comb hyperspectral digital holography for 3-D imaging
03

Nanophotonics & near-field nanoscopy

Scattering-type scanning near-field optical microscopy (s-SNOM) to image and control light far below the diffraction limit, with quantitative spectroscopic detail.

  • IR–THz nano-imaging of phonon- and plasmon-polaritons in van der Waals & polar materials
  • nano-FTIR and multicolor pseudoheterodyne near-field spectroscopy
  • Pump–probe nanospectroscopy of ultrafast carrier & phonon dynamics
  • Strong and ultrastrong light–matter coupling at the nanoscale

Signature contribution

Dual-comb hyperspectral digital holography

By interfering two frequency combs of slightly different repetition rates onto a lensless camera sensor, this technique records time-varying interference patterns that yield spectral hypercubes of complex holograms — amplitude and phase for every comb-line frequency. It enables scan-free wavefront reconstruction, long-range 3-D metrology without phase ambiguity, and molecule-selective hyperspectral imaging.

Vicentini, Wang, Van Gasse, Hänsch & Picqué, Nature Photonics 15, 890–894 (2021). I performed the experiments.

This work was recognised with the 2022 Helmholtz Prize (applied metrology) and the 2023 Bernard J. Couillaud Prize (Optica Foundation & Coherent).

Career journey

A progression from metrological precision at macroscopic scales toward quantitative measurement at the nanoscale.

  1. PhD & early career · Politecnico di Milano / CNR-IFN, Milan

    Laser sources & precision direct-comb spectroscopy

    Trained as a physics engineer in photonics and nano-optics, I carried out my doctoral research in the laser-physics and frequency-metrology group (with G. Galzerano and P. Laporta), developing mid- and near-infrared coherent sources and high-resolution molecular spectroscopy.

    • Ultrafast Cr:ZnSe mid-IR comb spectroscopy — Opt. Express (2023)
    • Absolute frequency stabilization of an 8.6 µm QCL — Opt. Lett. (2020)
    • Coherent mid-IR supercontinuum in suspended-core fibres — Opt. Lett. (2020)

  2. 2020–2021 · Max Planck Institute of Quantum Optics, Garching

    Dual-comb interferometry, imaging & holography

    As a visiting doctoral researcher with Nathalie Picqué in T. W. Hänsch’s Laser Spectroscopy Division, I moved into dual-comb interferometry and pioneered its application to coherent imaging.

    • Dual-comb hyperspectral digital holography — Nature Photonics (2021)
    • On-chip III–V-on-silicon laser comb for real-time gas spectroscopy
    • Time-resolved dual-comb spectroscopy with a single EO modulator — Opt. Lett. (2021)

  3. 2021–present · CIC nanoGUNE, San Sebastián

    Infrared near-field nanoscopy & ultrastrong coupling

    As a Juan de la Cierva postdoc in Rainer Hillenbrand’s Nanooptics group, I shifted from precision spectroscopy with frequency combs to quantitative near-field nano-optics, while remaining active in comb-based imaging.

    • Real-space flat-band ultrastrong coupling of phonons & plasmons — Nature Materials (2026)
    • Multicolor pseudoheterodyne near-field imaging — Opt. Express (2023)
    • 3-D hyperspectral imaging with optical microcombs — preprint (2025)
