About Me

I am a post‑doctoral researcher at CIC nanoGUNE in San Sebastián, Spain, working in the Nanooptics group led by Prof. Rainer Hillenbrand. My research focuses on developing advanced near‑field and pump‑probe nanospectroscopy techniques to study plasmonic and phononic materials at the nanoscale. With a background in physics engineering, ultrafast lasers and high‑resolution spectroscopy, I bridge engineering and fundamental science to push the frontiers of nano‑optics.

I obtained my Bachelor’s (2015) and Master’s (2017) degrees in Physics Engineering from the Polytechnic University of Milan, specializing in optics and photonics. In 2021 I earned a PhD in Physics with summa cum laude, supported by a doctoral grant to develop metrology‑grade spectrometers based on optical frequency combs. During this time I engineered ultrafast laser systems and precise spectroscopic models for applications ranging from water‑borne hazard detection to wine analysis.

My doctoral research at the Max Planck Institute of Quantum Optics, supervised by Dr. Nathalie Picqué in the group of Nobel laureate Prof. Theodor W. Hänsch, led to the demonstration of dual‑comb hyperspectral digital holography, published in Nature Photonics (2021). This breakthrough was recognized with the Helmholtz Prize (20 000 €) for precision measurement research.

Since joining nanoGUNE in late 2021, I have shifted towards nano‑optics and plasmonics. I received both a Juan de la Cierva Fellowship and a Marie Skłodowska‑Curie Individual Fellowship, securing over 240 000 € of funding to develop scattering‑type scanning near‑field optical microscopy (s‑SNOM) and nanospectroscopy techniques. My current work explores ultrastrong coupling phenomena between plasmonic and phononic materials using ultrafast pump‑probe s‑SNOM, opening new avenues for material science and photonic technologies.

To date I have authored 19 peer‑reviewed articles (9 as first author) in Q1 journals, including Nature Materials and Nature Photonics. My work has garnered an h‑index 10 with 360 citations (Google Scholar). I actively contribute to the scientific community through peer review for journals such as Nature, Nature Photonics and Optica, and by participating in prize committees and project evaluations.
