Short bio (50 words)

Edoardo Vicentini is a postdoctoral researcher at CIC nanoGUNE, developing ultrafast nano-spectroscopy and frequency-comb metrology. His work connects near-field imaging with femtosecond dynamics to understand and control light-matter interaction at surfaces.

Extended bio (150 words)

Edoardo Vicentini is a postdoctoral researcher in the Nanooptics Group at CIC nanoGUNE (San Sebastian, Spain). He develops ultrafast nano-spectroscopy and frequency-comb approaches to probe light-matter interaction at surfaces with nanometer spatial resolution and femtosecond timing. His research spans s-SNOM, nano-FTIR, dual-comb metrology, and polaritonic systems, with the goal of providing quantitative, traceable measurements for nanoscale materials. He earned his PhD in Physics from the Polytechnic University of Milan (cum laude) and has received the Marie Sklodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowship, the Juan de la Cierva Fellowship, and the Bernard J. Couillaud Prize in Ultrafast Lasers. He collaborates with the Max Planck Institute of Quantum Optics and CIC nanoGUNE on advanced instrumentation and ultrafast experiments.
